Welcome

Welcome to Matsusaka City, the modern city of mercantile, tradition, festivities, and comestibles.

Matsusaka is a city nearly at the heart of Mie Prefecture. Located in the south of Nagoya and northwest of Ise city. It is the home of about 158 thousand residents, sharing a capacious land area of 623.58 square kilometers.

The city offers a plethora of comfort and convenience. Local people find it easier to call the place Mattsaka, or Massaka. Matsusaka city is also the home of one of the most refined castles ever built in Japan, the Matsusaka castle. The influential feudal warlord, Gamou Ujisato commissioned the building of this magnificent castle in 1588, which was developed into a castle town in the Edo period. Ujisato invited excellent merchants all over Japan, and commerce has flourished since then. Matsusaka city became well-known and prominent as it huddled recognition throughout the country. The Ise Merchants inclusive of the Matsusaka area, Osaka Merchants, and Omi Merchants used to be classed the “Three Great Merchants of Japan.”

Zooming in through the lenses of the past, Matsusaka produced excellent merchants, sophisticated style, impressive intellect, and prodigious feat. The Hasegawa family opened a cotton shop in Edo in 1635. Their residence exhibited over thirty fashionable rooms, five exquisite storehouses, a stylish tatami room, an ornate tearoom, and a spacious Japanese garden. In addition, having an affection for the tea ceremony and Japanese poetry waka and haiku, the master of the Hasegawa family further cultivated his tastes by exchanging with and being a patron to legendary tea master Sen Soushitsu and the esteemed scholar of Japan literature, Motoori Norinaga.
